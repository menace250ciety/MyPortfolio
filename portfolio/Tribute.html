Dr. Norman Borlaug

The man who saved a billion of lives

Dr. Norman Borlaug with his achievements.

Dr. Norman Borlaug was a renowned scientist and humanitarian known for his contributions to agriculture and food security.

  • 1914: Norman Borlaug is born on March 25 in Cresco, Iowa, USA.
  • 1937: Borlaug graduates from the University of Minnesota with a Bachelor of Science in forestry.
  • 1942: He completes his Ph.D. in plant pathology and genetics at the University of Minnesota.
  • 1944-1960: Borlaug works as a research scientist in Mexico, where he begins his groundbreaking work on wheat breeding.
  • 1963: Dr. Borlaug and his team release the first semi-dwarf, high-yielding wheat varieties in Mexico, which become a part of the Green Revolution.
  • 1970: He is awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for his contributions to increasing global food production and reducing famine.
  • 1984: Dr. Borlaug establishes the World Food Prize, an international award recognizing individuals who have made significant contributions to improving the world's food supply.
  • 2006: He receives the Congressional Gold Medal, one of the highest civilian honors in the United States.
  • 2009: Dr. Norman Borlaug passes away on September 12 in Dallas, Texas, at the age of 95.
